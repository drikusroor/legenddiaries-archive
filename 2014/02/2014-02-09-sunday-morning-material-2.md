#### Sunday morning is a moment to recover from Saturday night and to catch up on some reading you missed during the week. Mainly because when you start gaming now your hangover will mix with motion sickness, which will not end well. So here some nice reading material to start the day with.

### [Gabe Newell on what makes Valve tick](http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-switch/wp/2014/01/03/gabe-newell-on-what-makes-valve-tick/)

### [Gabe Newell on Valve’s intimate relationship with its customers](http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-switch/wp/2014/01/06/gabe-newell-on-valves-intimate-relationship-with-its-customers/)

 

An interview with [Gabe Newell](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabe_Newell) in the Washington Post about how Valve works and how he sees the company. The interview is quite extensive and has been split into two parts. In this interview you get some interesting insight on how Valve works as a company, they do not track vacation days for example or hire people just because they have a Phd.

> Somebody having a PhD from an Ivy League school tells you nothing about whether they're going to be successful in our space. Grades don't tell you anything.

A must read for anyone who is interested in companies behind the games we play, but also interesting for those who have nothing with games because Valve is definitely an extraordinary company.
